W  ·  What I Understand

Brand built. Cold chain owned. Now the Platform becomes the bottleneck.

Licious has the trust, the supply chain, and the capital. The next 18 months — quick commerce defence, EBITDA discipline, and IPO readiness — will be decided inside the product surface. Five problems stand out from public reporting and real customer signal.

01

The quick commerce squeeze is real, and platform-deep

30-minute delivery is rolling out city by city. Zepto now ships Relish, Blinkit and Instamart are closing in. Roughly 15–20% of revenue already flows through partner apps. Defending the 85% on the Licious app means the experience there has to be visibly better — not just faster.

02

App reliability gaps are quietly leaking revenue

Public reviews flag payment glitches, slow loads, unexpected cancellations, and a buggy checkout. Each one is a lost cart on the channel that drives most of the business. This is the highest-leverage product surface in the company right now, and it isn't pretty enough yet.

03

Customer service friction is eating the trust the brand built

Refund delays, missing items, unreachable agents, ticket loops — review sentiment skews unfavourable on consumer forums. A brand built on trust loses more from one bad complaint than it earns from ten great deliveries. This is solvable with platform, not headcount.

04

Cold chain & dark stores are scaling faster than the data layer

50+ branded stores, IoT-powered cold chain at 0–4°C across 28 cities, quick commerce dark stores, partner platforms. The operational surface is growing fast. Without one unified telemetry, inventory and SLA layer, every new node adds complexity instead of leverage.

05

The IPO clock is now the product clock

Monthly burn cut from ~₹22 Cr to ~₹8 Cr. Profitability is the priority, listing pushed to FY27–28. Every platform inefficiency — tickets per order, refund rate, stock-outs, repeat-order drop-off — now shows up on the EBITDA path. Product priorities have to live on the same lens.
